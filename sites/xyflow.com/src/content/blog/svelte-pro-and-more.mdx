We’ve been working hard this spring, and today we’re introducing Svelte Pro, alongside a
wave of major updates across the platform, including a new, fully integrated Pro
experience, long-awaited Dark Mode, and countless improvements make everything more
cohesive, and smoother to use.

### Introducing Svelte Flow Pro

[React Flow Pro](https://reactflow.dev/pro) has been our paid offering for a few
years now, providing access to advanced examples while supporting the ongoing
development of our libraries. We’re excited to now expand that offering to our
Svelte Flow users with the launch of [Svelte Flow Pro](https://svelteflow.dev/pro).

We’ve been continually impressed by what our Svelte Flow users have been building,
and we’re looking forward to see folks take their apps to the next level with
in-depth, production-ready examples.

Each Pro Example is an independent Vite project showing off advanced techniques
or tricky features, with production-ready well-documented code you can integrate 
directly into your own projects. We always love seeing people are making with our
libraries, so if you’ve built something you are a proud of please submit your
project to our [showcase](https://svelteflow.dev/showcase).

You can get access to a free Pro Example just by [signing up](https://svelteflow.dev/pro/sign-up).
And if you’re already a React Flow Pro subscriber? Great news! **One subscription**
grants you access to the Pro content for _all_ of our libraries. 

### The new Pro Platform

With the addition of Svelte Flow Pro, we’ve also taken the opportunity to overhaul
the Pro Platform itself. Back in 2023 we [open sourced the Pro Platform](/blog/react-flow-pro-platform-open-source)
so folks could adapt it for their own open source projects.

Isolating the Pro Platform this way made it easy to share, but more-difficult to
integrate into our main content sites: navigating to the Pro Platform would mean
leaving `reactflow.dev` and signing in to `pro.reactflow.dev`. Going forward we
want to provide a more seamless experience for accessing Pro content, meaning Pro
content for both React Flow and Svelte Flow will be accessible directly from the
main documentation sites.

For now that means the [Pro Platform repo](https://github.com/xyflow/pro-platform)
will be archived and no longer maintained. The repo will remain publicly available
for folks to fork or continue to learn from, but don’t expect any updates or support 
for it going forward.

### Dark mode (finally)

A long awaited sight for sore eyes: dark mode now available across all our sites
and examples. Along the way we’ve given everything a face lift, improved color
consistency, and made accessibility improvements to ensure everything looks and
feels great in both modes.

If you have a penchant for caffeine-fueled late-night coding sessions, this one’s
for you. <Emoji content="🫡" />

### And more

Alongside the big updates, we’ve also picked up our dirty laundry, swept the floors,
and made a wide range of improvements across the platform:

- cleaner navigation with improved menus
- better account handling
- smoother buttons, animations, and transitions
- major improvements to the codebase that make the site easier to navigate and explore.

These updates are all about making the platform more unified, more polished, and more
enjoyable to use. If you spot something we missed, or have suggestions on how to
make the docs experience even better please let us know by [opening an issue](https://github.com/xyflow/web/issues)
on GitHub or reaching out on our [Discord server](https://discord.com/invite/RVmnytFmGW).

As always, there’s more to come.
